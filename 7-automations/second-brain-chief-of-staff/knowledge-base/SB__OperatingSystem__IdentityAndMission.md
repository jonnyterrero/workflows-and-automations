## Context
- Profile: Bioengineering major; minors in Computer Science and Chemistry.
- Roles: Student-researcher, software builder, strength athlete, Orthodox Christian.
- Current season: Build a rigorous second-brain workflow that improves execution quality and reduces cognitive overload.

## Key Ideas
- Primary mission: Build a life of disciplined competence and service through science, engineering, and faith.
- Identity standard: Be reliable, technically sharp, physically prepared, spiritually grounded.
- Strategic posture: Choose fewer high-value commitments and execute deeply.
- Decision filter: Prefer options that compound skill, trust, and long-term leverage.
- Anti-drift rule: If an activity does not support mission or recovery, it must be reduced or removed.

## Decisions
- Prioritize depth over breadth in semester commitments.
- Preserve a weekly Sabbath window and liturgical obligations.
- Keep 3 non-negotiables daily: coursework progress, training/recovery, spiritual rule.
- Require documented rationale for adding any new project.

## Constraints
- Weekly schedule capacity is finite (~55 focused hours including classes, labs, and study).
- High CNS demand from powerlifting and BJJ limits late-night deep work frequency.
- Lab and course deadlines are externally fixed.
- Personal energy dips after 8:30 PM; planning should front-load hard cognition.

## Open Questions
- Which commitments create the most mission-aligned return this semester?
- Where is perfectionism creating hidden procrastination?
- What should be paused for 8 weeks to unlock deep progress?

## Next Actions
- Write a one-page mission scorecard and review every Sunday.
- Rank current commitments by strategic value and eliminate bottom 20%.
- Define explicit minimums for faith, academics, athletics, and project execution.
