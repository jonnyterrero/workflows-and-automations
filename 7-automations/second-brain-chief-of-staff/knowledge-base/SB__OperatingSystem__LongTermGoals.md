## Context
- Horizon 1 (0-12 months): Academic excellence, systemization, and portfolio credibility.
- Horizon 2 (1-3 years): Research publication trajectory + strong software/AI engineering competence.
- Horizon 3 (3-7 years): Leadership in biomedical innovation with technical and ethical depth.

## Key Ideas
- H1 outcomes: GPA target >=3.7, reproducible study/research system, 2 high-quality project artifacts.
- H2 outcomes: Competitive profile for PhD/MS or R&D roles; one significant research contribution.
- H3 outcomes: Build/lead products that improve patient outcomes and care workflows.
- Capital strategy: Invest in rare skill intersections (wet-lab literacy + computation + systems thinking).

## Decisions
- Prioritize roles/projects that sharpen quantitative modeling, experimental design, and software shipping.
- Preserve time for foundational math/CS upskilling each semester.
- Treat communication skill (writing/presentation) as a core technical multiplier.

## Constraints
- Financial constraints may limit unpaid research opportunities.
- Time competition between coursework, research, athletics, and product development.
- Geographic and mentorship availability can shape next-stage options.

## Open Questions
- Which graduate pathways best match bioengineering + AI translational interests?
- What evidence is still missing for a top-tier research application package?
- Which current projects are portfolio-grade vs learning-only?

## Next Actions
- Build a 12-month milestone map with quarterly checkpoints.
- Create a target-lab and mentor list with fit criteria.
- Define one flagship project narrative bridging bioengineering and software.
