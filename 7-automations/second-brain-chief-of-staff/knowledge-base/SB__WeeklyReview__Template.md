## Context
- Review cadence: Sunday evening (45-60 min).
- Goal: identify what moved, what stalled, and what must change next week.

## Key Ideas
- Scoreboard sections:
  - Wins with evidence (what shipped, submitted, improved).
  - Misses with root causes (capacity error, unclear scope, avoidance, dependency).
  - Priority alignment check (did time match stated priorities?).
  - Energy/recovery audit (sleep, training load, cognitive quality).
- Planning output: top 3 outcomes for next week + blocked-time calendar draft.

## Decisions
- Non-negotiable questions:
  1) What mattered most and did it actually get done?
  2) What did I avoid and why?
  3) What must be cut next week to stay realistic?
- Require at least one systems improvement every review (process, tooling, environment).

## Constraints
- Avoid excessive introspection without behavioral change.
- Keep review to 1 page unless major project pivot is needed.
- Do not add more than 3 weekly priorities.

## Open Questions
- Which recurring bottleneck appeared again this week?
- Did commitments exceed available deep-work capacity?
- Where did standards slip and what correction is needed?

## Next Actions
- Fill review using factual evidence (calendar, tasks, submissions).
- Commit next week priorities before ending review.
- Schedule Monday first deep-work block immediately.
